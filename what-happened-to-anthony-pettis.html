What Happened to Anthony Pettis?

August 05, 2024
blog

Anthony Pettis, a former UFC and WEC lightweight champion, has had a storied journey in the world of mixed martial arts (MMA) and boxing.

From his early successes in the UFC to his recent foray into professional boxing, Pettis has remained a prominent figure in combat sports.

Early Career and UFC Success

Anthony Pettis gained widespread recognition during his time in the UFC, where he became the UFC lightweight champion, earning acclaim for his distinctive fighting style and impressive victories. His notable fights, including his victory against Benson Henderson at UFC 164, solidified his status as a top-tier MMA athlete.

Transition to Professional Fighters League (PFL)

After parting ways with the UFC, Pettis made a significant career move by joining the Professional Fighters League (PFL) in 2021.

His debut in the PFL was highly anticipated, and he headlined the 2021 PFL season opener against Clay Collard.

Despite facing ups and downs in the PFL, Pettis continued to showcase his resilience and determination in the league.

Boxing Debut and Return to MMA

In 2023, Pettis made his professional boxing debut, securing a victory against former four-division boxing champion, Roy Jones Jr. 

This marked a pivotal moment in Pettis' career, demonstrating his versatility and willingness to explore new avenues in combat sports.

Despite his success in boxing, Pettis expressed his intention to return to MMA, emphasizing that he still has unfinished business in the cage.

Notable Fights and Achievements

Throughout his career, Pettis has been involved in several high-profile fights, earning accolades such as the "Performance of the Night" and "Fight of the Night" awards in the UFC. His victories and memorable performances against renowned opponents have contributed to his legacy in MMA.

Challenges and Determination

Pettis has faced challenges, including injuries and the rigors of weight cutting in the PFL, but his resolve to overcome obstacles and compete at the highest level has remained unwavering. 

Future Prospects

As Pettis navigates his combat sports journey, his return to the MMA arena and potential participation in the PFL's pay-per-view model in 2024 are highly anticipated.

His ability to adapt to different disciplines, coupled with his competitive spirit, positions him as a compelling figure in the world of combat sports.

 

All in all, Pettis' transition from the UFC to the PFL, his successful boxing debut, and his enduring passion for MMA underscore his resilience and determination as a fighter.

With a career marked by remarkable victories and an unyielding pursuit of excellence, Pettis continues to captivate fans and inspire aspiring athletes in the realm of combat sports.
